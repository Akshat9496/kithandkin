What We Do

A concierge service built on
trust, not technology.

I

Structured Home Visits

Regular, scheduled in-person visits. Consistent, never missed, personally supervised by the founder.

II

Health Monitoring

BP and pulse checked and logged every visit. Medication adherence observed. Early warning signs identified and flagged.

III

Family Kept Informed

Same-day WhatsApp report after every visit — vitals, medication, mood, and any issues flagged. You always know.

IV

Errands & Coordination

Bills, groceries, home repairs, banking, hospital appointments — we coordinate so nothing falls through the cracks.

Non-medical monitoring only. Kith & Kin is a companionship and concierge service — not a nursing agency or medical provider. We observe, report, coordinate and accompany. WhatsApp support available 7am–10pm daily.
A Typical Visit

What a visit looks like.

Every visit follows the same structured format. Here is what happens from arrival to the moment you receive your report.

1
On arrival
Greet & Settle
We arrive at the agreed time, greet your parent warmly, and take a moment to check how they are feeling before anything else.
2
5–10 mins in
Vital Checks
Blood pressure and pulse taken and logged. Medication confirmed present and taken as scheduled. Any visible concerns noted.
3
Middle of visit
Conversation & Wellbeing
Structured conversation covering how they have been, mood, appetite, sleep, and social activity. Depth varies by plan — from a focused 15-minute wellbeing check-in on Essential, to a 30–40 minute mood assessment on Signature, to a full 45–60 minute longitudinal assessment on Premiere.
4
As needed
Errands & Tasks
Bills, groceries, repair coordination, banking — any agreed tasks handled. Signature and Premiere plans include this as standard.
5
Before leaving
Wrap Up
We confirm the next visit, check if anything is needed, and make sure your parent is settled and comfortable before we leave.
6
Same day
WhatsApp Report
You receive a structured report within a few hours — vitals, medication status, mood, and any issues flagged. Nothing left out.

The founder conducts every onboarding visit personally, makes a monthly check-in with every client, and oversees all reporting. With a deliberately small client circle, every family is known by name — not by account number.

Before You Begin

Your Questions, Answered

We know you are trusting us with the person who matters most. These are the questions every family asks — and deserves a straight answer to.

Visits are by trained, verified associates supervised by the founder. The founder personally conducts every onboarding visit and makes a monthly check-in with every client.
Every associate undergoes police verification and structured training. The founder’s background and references are available on request. The first visit is always by the founder personally.
On the same day as every visit, your family WhatsApp group receives a structured report: vitals, medication, mood observations, and issues flagged. Signature and Premiere members also get proactive updates between visits.
We are the first call. We notify your family immediately, coordinate with your doctor or hospital, and stay on-site until stable. Premiere members receive a written emergency protocol at onboarding.
Plans start from ₹5,999 per month. Payment is collected before your first visit. No free trials — but the founder’s onboarding visit is included in your first month so you see exactly how we work from day one.
We coordinate a comprehensive home collection covering 80+ health parameters. The founder is present and the report is shared with the family with a plain-language summary. Included after 6 months on Signature, 3 months on Premiere.
Onboarding takes 5–7 days from confirmed booking. No long-term contract. Cancel anytime with 7 days notice before your next billing date. We raise your invoice 7 days before each month begins. We’d rather earn your loyalty than lock it in.
Every week we call your parent for a structured conversation — how they are feeling, whether any needs have come up, anything the family should know. It is not a medical call. It is a consistent, caring presence that catches small issues before they become big ones.
